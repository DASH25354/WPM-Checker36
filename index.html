【36】On December 10, Australia introduced a world-first law banning social media for children under 16. The goal is to protect kids from addiction, cyberbullying, and dangerous people online. The whole world is watching this strict new rule. 　Ten major platforms, including Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and X, are included in the ban. These companies must use age-verification technology to identify and freeze accounts of users under 16. Most companies agreed to follow the law, even though they doubt it will effectively make children safer. 　Prime Minister Albanese called it a "proud day" for Australia. He stated that families are taking control back from big tech companies. He wants children to enjoy their childhood and parents to have peace of mind. However, he admitted implementing this will not be easy. 　Under this law, social media companies must take "reasonable steps" to stop under-16s from using their apps. If they fail, they face fines of up to 49.5 million Australian dollars. Importantly, there are no penalties for children or parents who break the rules.
