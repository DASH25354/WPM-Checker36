Three-minute Training

【32】There is a Japanese saying: “Kindness to others is never wasted.” It means this: when we are kind to someone, it helps that person, and someday the kindness may come back to us in another way. We do not need to expect a reward. Kind actions can make our heart and our time rich. 　Let me share simple examples. You lend a pen to a classmate who forgets theirs. Your pen is away for a short time, but your classmate can write and feels calm. Later, you forget your umbrella on a rainy day. Another classmate offers to share an umbrella with you. Your small help returns to you. 　Another example: You share half of your bread with a friend who is hungry. Yes, your bread becomes less. But you enjoy eating together, and your bond grows stronger. Kindness can make friendship warm and strong. 　Kindness is not about harming yourself or always giving up your needs. It is about a gentle heart and a wish to help in a useful way. One day, I helped a friend with a hard problem. I listened and gave ideas. I forgot about it. Years later, I had a big problem. I did not ask anyone, but that friend sent me a message at the right time. We met again after many years. The friend listened to me and gave very good advice. I felt lucky. It was like a gift. 　Kindness may not return soon, and it may not return from the same person. So do not wait or worry. Live with quiet hope. Kindness moves in circles, and it often comes back when we most need it.
